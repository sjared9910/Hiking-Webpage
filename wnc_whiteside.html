Whiteside Mountain Trail

Whiteside Mountain Trail is a 2.5 mile round trip that follows the 700ft high cliffs to the 4930 ft summit. This trail offers amazing mountain views for hikers with cliffs topped with mountain laurel. This trail climbs an old road along the spectacular cliffs of Whiteside Mountain to the summit where you will find an amazing view of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

This moderately difficult trail starts from the Whiteside Mountain parking area and begins fairly steep up to an old roadbed. Following the slightly steep roadbed will bring will bring you to a fork in the path. To the left is an easier but longer ascent to the summit while to the right is a shorter and steeper ascent. Due to the high elevation, high winds and cold temperatures can occur. Plan accordingly to weather and season.

Trail Details

Difficulty: Moderate

Length: 2.5 miles round trip

Climb: Moderate

Lowest Elevation: 4410ft

Highest Elevation: 4930ft

Total Climb: 600ft

Starting Point: Whiteside Mountain Parking Area

Where to Find

This trail is located within the Nantahala Ranger District area. Starting in Highlands NC, follow US 64 East for 5.4 miles. Look for a sign for the Whiteside Mountain Recreation Area and turn onto Whiteside Mt. Rd. Follow this road for 1 mile and there will be a pay both. The trail begins from the parking area.

The Grandfather Trail

The Grandfather Trail is located within Grandfather Mountain State Park and is a 2 mile trail up to Calloway Peak. This trail begins with traveling through a spruce forest, up and around rock walls and up the ridge to Calloway Peak. This trail is not for faint of heart but those who choose to complete it are rewarded with one of the greatest views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

The Grandfather Trail is one of the most difficult trails that Western North Carolina has to offer. This 2 mile trail is not for inexperienced hikers. This trail requires extinsive bouldering and climbing multiple ladders in steep sections. Due to the high elevation, extremely high wind gusts can occur making a hard trail even more difficult. Despite being a tourist attraction, do not under estimate this trail!

Trail Details

Difficulty: Most Difficult

Length: 4.1 miles out and back

Climb: Difficult

Lowest Elevation: 5250 ft

Highest Elevation: 5946 ft

Total Climb: 1341 ft

Starting Point: Black Rock Parking Area

Where to Find

Grandfather Mountain State Park is located between Asheville and Boone North Carolina. For a more scenic drive, take the Blue Ridge Parkway across the Lin Cove Viaduct. US 221 North from Linville NC provides a more direct way to the entrance of the State Park.

Fryingpan Mountain Tower

Fryingpan Mountain Tower is a 1.5 mile out and back trail that takes you out to an old fire lookout tower at the summit of Fryingpan Mountain. This tower rises up 70ft to give you a breathtaking view of the Blue Ridge Mountains and the Pisgah National Forest. This tower gives a fantastic view of nearby peaks of Looking Glass Rock, Mt. Pisgah, and Cold Mountain.

This short hike takes you from the Blue Ridge Parkway up an old forest service road to the tower and the mountain summit. This road is a slight but continuouses climb to the top. The trail being a road makes it very wide and easy to follow and is well maintained. Due to the high elevation, high winds and cold temperatures can occur. Plan accordingly to weather and season.

Trail Details

Difficulty: Moderate

Length: 1.5 miles out and back

Climb: Moderate

Lowest Elevation: 4983ft

Highest Elevation: 5340ft

Total Climb: 357ft

Starting Point: Blue Ridge Parkway milepost 409, Forest Service Road 409

Where to Find

To get to Fryingpan Mountain Tower, take the Blue Ridge Parkway South from downtown Asheville to milepost 409. There is no parking area so you will have to park on the side of the road near Forest Service Road 450. The gate will always be closed, just walk around it.

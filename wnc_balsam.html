Black Balsam Knob via the Art Loeb Trail

Take a hike to Black Balsam Knob Recreation Area via the Art Loeb Trail where you will see some of the most spectacular mountain balds in the Appalachians. The treeless mountaintops allows for some of the best views of the the Blue Ridge Moountains. This area features many other bald mountaintops and hiking trails for you to explore.

While there are many ways to get to Black Balsam Knob, the easist is via the Art Loeb Trail. Here you will find an 0.7 mile trails going to the summit of Tennent mountain. This trail is moderatly steep with a total climb of 425ft. Other trail options are avaible that are longer and less steep. Due to the high elevation, high winds and cold temperatures can occur. Plan accordingly to weather and season.

Trail Details

Difficulty: Moderate

Length: 1.5 miles out and back

Climb: challenging

Lowest Elevation: 5789ft

Highest Elevation: 6214ft

Total Climb: 425ft

Starting Point:Black Balsam parking lot at Blue Ridge Parkway milepost 420

Where to Find

To get to Black Balsam Knob via the Art Loeb Trail, take the Blue Ridge Parkway south from downtown Asheville to milepost 420. Here you will see a parking area for the Art Loeb trailhead.
